Abstract. GIFT-64 is a 64-bit block cipher with a 128-bit key that is
more lightweight than PRESENT. This paper provides a detailed analysis of
GIFT-64 against diÔ¨Äerential and linear attacks. Our work complements
automatic search methods for the best diÔ¨Äerential and linear charac-
teristics with a careful manual analysis. This hybrid approach leads to
new insights. In the diÔ¨Äerential setting, we theoretically explain the exis-
tence of diÔ¨Äerential characteristics with two active S-boxes per round
and derive some novel properties of these characteristics. Furthermore,
we prove that all optimal diÔ¨Äerential characteristics of GIFT-64 covering
more than seven rounds must activate two S-boxes per round. We can
construct all optimal characteristics by hand. In parallel to the work in
the diÔ¨Äerential setting, we conduct a similar analysis in the linear setting.
However, unlike the clear view in diÔ¨Äerential setting, the optimal linear
characteristics of GIFT-64 must have at least one round activating only
one S-box. Moreover, with the assistance of automatic searching meth-
ods, we identify 24 GIFT-64 variants achieving better resistance against
diÔ¨Äerential attack while maintaining a similar security level against a lin-
ear attack. Since the new variants strengthen GIFT-64 against statistical
cryptanalysis, we claim that the number of rounds could be reduced from
28 to 26 for the variants. This observation enables us to create a cipher
with lower energy consumption than GIFT-64. Similarly to the case in
GIFT-64, we do not claim any related-key security for the round-reduced
variant as this is not relevant for most applications.

which intends to determine if there exists an instantiation that satisÔ¨Āes a given
Boolean formula. In practice, we transform cryptanalytic problems into SAT
problems and employ the same SAT solver CiDiCaL [8] as in [30] to solve all
concerned SAT problems.

approach to generate diÔ¨Äerential and linear models of a given S-box.

Conclusion and Future Work
6.1
Conclusion
This paper targets the cryptanalysis of GIFT-64 and combines automatic and
manual methods to evaluate its security. In the diÔ¨Äerential setting, we theoreti-
cally explain the existence of diÔ¨Äerential characteristics with two active S-boxes
per round and derive some properties of these characteristics, apart from the
quantitative information about active S-boxes. Furthermore, all optimal diÔ¨Äer-
ential characteristics covering more than seven rounds are identiÔ¨Āed. Parallel
work is conducted in the linear setting. Considering the gap between the upper
bounds on the diÔ¨Äerential probability and the linear correlation, we study a
variant of GIFT-64 with comparable security levels in the diÔ¨Äerential and linear
settings. With the support of automatic searching methods, we identify 24 vari-
ants achieving better resistance against diÔ¨Äerential cryptanalysis than GIFT-64
while maintaining a similar security level against linear cryptanalysis. As the
new variants strengthen GIFT-64 against statistical cryptanalysis, we claim that
for the variant GIFT-64[gc
0], if the security in the related-key attack setting is
not required, 26 rounds could be used rather than 28 rounds. This observation
results in a cipher more suitable for the low-energy consumption use cases than

A Greater GIFT
141
GIFT-64. The performance of the 24 variants in the related-key diÔ¨Äerential attack
setting is inferior to that of GIFT-64. However, most applications do not need
related-key security.
6.2
Future Work
If one is concerned with related-key attacks, we conjecture that the resistance
of variants in GIFT-64[2021] regarding related-key diÔ¨Äerential attack can be
lifted by carefully crafting the key schedule. However, many parameters should
be Ô¨Āne-tuned. Thus, we left it as future work.
Secondly, in the construction of GIFT-64-like cipher, we apply the same 16-
bit group mapping to each column of the state. How to eÔ¨Éciently evaluate the
cases where the group mappings operating on diÔ¨Äerent columns are distinct is
an open problem.
Lastly, for GIFT-128, the security levels regarding diÔ¨Äerential and linear
cryptanalyses are also not comparable. We attempt to create an equivalence
relation among all variants for GIFT-128. Nevertheless, the number of equiva-
lence classes is 1344. In addition, due to the considerable state size, investigat-
ing the security of the variants for GIFT-128 is much more complicated than
that of GIFT-64. Still, considering the signiÔ¨Ācant status of GIFT-128 among the
lightweight block ciphers and its supporting role in a series of Authenticated
Encryptions with Associated Data (AEADs), especially in one of the Ô¨Ānalists
GIFT-COFB [3] of NIST Lightweight Cryptography project3, we believe check-
ing the existence of a balanced variant for GIFT-128 will be interesting future
work. For more details about the test of GIFT-128, see Supplementary Mate-
rial F of the long version.
