On the Use of Non-standard Lattice Assumptions. As mentioned earlier
in the introduction, our ABE with short ciphertexts are a substantial improve-
ment over the state of the art of ABE from standard LWE. In fact, starting
from standard LWE, we do not even know how to build ABE with o(в„“)-sized
ciphertexts for very simple circuits, such as linear or NC0 functionalities (e.g., the
index function, which corresponds to broadcast encryption); this is the case even
if we allow large secret keys. One way to understand this phenomenon is to look
at pairing-based ABE, where all known approaches for o(в„“)-sized ciphertexts
require q-type assumptions, short of relying on dual system encryption [46].
If we move beyond LWE, then one natural question is, what is the simplest
useful non-standard lattice assumption? A natural starting point would be to
rely on evasive LWE for a speciп¬Ғc distribution of вҖңhintsвҖқ BвҲ’1(P). Following

On в„“-succinct LWE vs evasive LWE. As mentioned earlier in the introduc-

better than relying on evasive LWE. Here, we point out two additional techni-

the results and techniques in this work, would prompt the research community
to move towards the use of falsiп¬Ғable lattice assumptions like в„“-succinct LWE,
instead of evasive LWE.
Gaining Conп¬Ғdence in в„“-succinct LWE. We begin by noting that all known
attacks on LWE have a SIS analogue, so we will treat в„“-succinct LWE and
в„“-succinct SIS somewhat interchangeably. Given that в„“-succinct LWE and в„“-
succinct SIS are respectively weaker than evasive LWE and BASISstruct (and the
closely related k-R-SIS) used in prior works ([2,3,44,45,47,48] for the former,
[8,10,24,28,49,50] for the latter), up to polynomial losses in parameters, this
gives us signiп¬Ғcant conп¬Ғdence in в„“-succinct LWE.

Related Works Based on Obfuscation. [38] showed that assuming iO, we
can get вҖңoptimalвҖқ ABE (and even FE) for circuits with |mpk|, |ct|, |sk| = poly(О»).
Two recent works on LFE from obfuscation (for Turing machines and RAM

size (1 + o(1)) В· в„“.
Pairing-Based Schemes. We note that our compressed LWE sample shares
a similar algebraic structure to the constant-size ciphertext in the pairing-
based ABE schemes in [9,12,14] as well as the constant-size ciphertext in the
second BGGHNSVV ABE based on multi-linear maps. However, the way we
exploit this structure is very diп¬Җerent and has no analogue in the pairings set-
ting. In fact, naively translating our technique to the group-based setting would
require at least trilinear maps, since the ciphertext, trapdoor basis, and secret
keys would need to be encoded in three separate groups.
Improving on the Depth Dependency. Two recent works [25,37] improved
on the dependency on d in existing ABE and LFE schemes, replacing several
poly(d, О») factors with poly(О») factors; these improvements are orthogonal to the
ones in this work, which focuses on the dependency on в„“, and rely on completely
diп¬Җerent techniques. In particular, for NC1 circuits, these works do not improve
on the state-of-the-art from LWE, whereas we do. We defer the natural question
of getting the best of both worlds to future work.

of functional commitments for circuits with fast veriп¬Ғcation based on в„“-succinct
LWE, starting from the compression mechanism introduced in this work. (An
earlier version of this work of this work containing only the results for Оұ = 1 was

Targets for Crypt-Analysis
We suggest the following intermediate targets as open problems for cryptanalysis
corresponding to potential improvements over attacking SIS/LWE directly, as a
step towards understanding hardness:
вҖ“ An attack on в„“-succinct SIS/LWE that exploits T to obtain poly(в„“) speed-up

analogue to CheonвҖҷs attack on q-type assumptions in pairings [22])
вҖ“ An attack with complexity exponential in ЛҶm, where ЛҶm is the width of W
(c.f. Section 3). Indeed, when ЛҶm = 0, we have a trapdoor for Iв„“вҠ—B, which
breaks the assumption.
These targets are consistent with the following facts: в„“-succinct SIS/LWE
assumption becomes stronger as ЛҶm decreases and as в„“increases, via standard
lattice delegation [21]. We stress that these targets are far from contradicting
polynomial hardness of в„“-succinct LWE with sub-exponential modulus-to-noise
ratio, which is what we need for our results.
